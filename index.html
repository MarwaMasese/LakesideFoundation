About

Providing Sanitary towels for girls &women

The Pads Mashinani idea began when Nyanza health team took their time to survey the challenges our young vulnerable women endure on their day today lives while struggling for their daily bread. There they learned about the issue of girls in Mashinani settlements are missing or dropping out of school with the onset of menstruation. They also learned and noticed the alarming outspread of UTIs and other conditions they face besides lacking the menstrual pads. They were so inspired by these stories that they decided to shine a spotlight on this issue by sourcing the sanitary towel and distributing to various groups in Mashinani.

OUR PROGRAMS

To accomplish our core mandate, our organisation undertakes the following programs among the vulnerable population

PADS FOR ALL

Students in Mashinani often have difficulty obtaining menstrual hygiene products, and because of the pandemic, access is more limited than ever. There is need also to provide free menstrual hygiene products in school restrooms, many students have no or lost access to a reliable source of menstrual supplies. That’s why The Pad Mashinani Project is sourcing for this towels and distributing them to schools, communities and villages.

SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS

The Pad Project’s work to empower women and girls around the Migori County is in line with the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). We are working to promote the health and wellbeing of girls and women by increasing access to affordable, hygienic menstrual products and by offering MHM and SRHR workshops. By providing menstrual products we are also helping students stay in school, which allows them to receive a quality education.

Ambassador Program

The Pad Mashinani Project’s Ambassador Program is designed to unite a community of passionate individuals from around the world who are ready to break the stigma surrounding menstruation and turn their passion for equity into action. The Ambassador Program fosters a way for new and life-long menstrual equity advocates to come together to learn about period poverty, develop leadership skills, and form new friendships.

Local Ownership

We exercise meaningful engagement of our stakeholders in the development and implementation of our programs. Integrated Programming: To ensure comprehensive support to clients and beneficiaries, our programs deliberately respond to the relevant social, behavioral, biomedical and structural drivers of HIV infection and transmission.
